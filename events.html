Previous Events

Some of the previous events planned by Planet Over Profit

On Saturday July 11th 2020, we gathered in Memorial park at dusk to commemorate the 30 year anniversary of the Mohawk Standoff, otherwise known as the Oka crisis. A circle of about 40 of us gathered to remember this standoff as an example of strength, as a win that brought relief to many- but not as the end. The federal government continues to claim ownership over most sovereign Indigenous lands, including the land disputed during the 1990 standoff. On this anniversary 30 years later, we are still seeing government sanctioned police violence for sovereign Indigenous territory. We stand in solidarity with the Wet'suwet'en, who continue to be terrorized by the armed, unwelcome presence of RCMP, while construction of the country's largest pipelines continues across their territory- without the consent of the Wet'suwet'en hereditary chiefs.
